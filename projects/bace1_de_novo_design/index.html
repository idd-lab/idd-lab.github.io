De Novo BACE1 Inhibitor Design

Integrating Reinforcement Learning and Genetic Algorithms for Alzheimer's therapeutics.

**Status:** Preprint Available (ChemRxiv) | In Revision for *Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling (JCIM)* This project targets **Beta-secretase 1 (BACE1)**, the rate-limiting enzyme in the generation of amyloid-$\beta$ peptides. Traditional discovery pipelines have failed to balance blood-brain barrier (BBB) permeability with the selectivity required for this aspartic protease. Addressing the exhaustion of existing chemical libraries, the IDD Lab developed a dual-pronged generative framework. We synthesized the predictive power of **Reinforcement Learning (RL)** and **Genetic Algorithms (GA)** to explore novel chemical space, validated by rigorous **Molecular Dynamics (MD)** simulations. ### Key Resources * Read the Preprint (ChemRxiv) * View Data Repository (GitHub) --- ### Methodology Our "hybrid" approach integrates Generative Deep Learning (Ligand-Based) and Evolutionary Algorithms (Structure-Based): 1. **Ligand-Based Generative AI:** * Developed an **LSTM-based generative model** fine-tuned via **Reinforcement Learning**. * Guided by a **QSAR Ensemble Reward Function** ($R^2 = 0.67$, $RMSE = 0.65$) trained on 2,237 BindingDB inhibitors. * Generated 3,000 valid molecules with high novelty ($Tanimoto < 0.4$). 2. **Structure-Based Evolutionary Design:** * Utilized **AutoGrow4** (Genetic Algorithm) to evolve fragments within the BACE1 active site. * Implemented a **Size-Independent Ligand Efficiency (SILE)** scoring function to mitigate molecular size bias, producing 5,020 pocket-compatible compounds. 3. **Rigorous Validation Pipeline:** * **Docking:** Exhaustive screening against Human $\beta$-secretase 1 (**PDB: 4XXS**) using AutoDock Vina. * **Lead Optimization:** Rational bioisosteric replacement ($N \rightarrow C$) to optimize TPSA for BBB permeability. * **Molecular Dynamics:** Performed triplicate **100ns MD simulations** (GROMACS) with MM/GBSA binding free energy estimation. ### Key Findings * **Compound 14XS (Optimized Lead):** Achieved a binding free energy of $\Delta G_{bind} = -39.47$ kcal/mol. Validated as a BBB-permeable, non-canonical binder stabilizing the "flap" region (Tyr76/Thr77). * **Compound 80 (LBDD Hit):** Demonstrated high affinity ($\Delta G_{bind} = -33.17$ kcal/mol) by anchoring to the hydrophobic S3 sub-pocket (Arg133/Trp81). * **False Positive Detection:** MD simulations exposed "canonical" binder Compound 32 as a false positive (hydrophobic only), highlighting the necessity of dynamic validation over static docking. --- ### Citation If you use this data or methodology, please cite: > Dang, T. T., & Luong, M. N. (2025). *Integrating Structure-Based Genetic Algorithms and Reinforcement Learning for De Novo Discovery of Novel BACE1 Inhibitors*. ChemRxiv. DOI: 10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-bj970
